# Overview

## About This Section

This section provides the foundational context for understanding natural language processing and the evolution of language AI. It covers the progression from rule-based systems to modern large language models, the fundamental challenges that make NLP difficult, and the paradigm shifts that have transformed how we approach language understanding and generation.

Understanding the historical context and core challenges of NLP before diving into technical details helps you appreciate why certain architectures and approaches exist, and why working with language models requires different thinking than traditional software systems.

## Contents

### [Evolution of NLP](evolution-of-nlp.md)

The journey from rule-based systems to statistical methods to neural networks to large language models. Covers the major paradigm shifts in NLP: symbolic AI → statistical NLP → neural NLP → pre-trained transformers → LLMs. Understanding this progression reveals why modern approaches work and what problems they solve.

### [Language Understanding Challenges](language-challenges.md)

The fundamental difficulties that make natural language processing hard: ambiguity, context dependence, world knowledge requirements, compositional semantics, and pragmatics. Explores why language is inherently complex and why even state-of-the-art models still struggle with certain phenomena.

### [The LLM Paradigm Shift](llm-paradigm-shift.md)

How large language models changed everything. Covers the move from task-specific models to general-purpose language models, the pretrain-prompt paradigm, emergent abilities at scale, and the implications for how we build NLP systems. Understanding this shift is critical for working effectively with modern language AI.
